The Problem

Financial services has a gap.

Big firms want clients with $500,000. Robo-advisors race to the bottom on price, treating everyone the same. In between sits the HENRY—High Earner, Not Rich Yet. Too wealthy for basic tools. Not wealthy enough for white-glove service.

Stash Wealth built a high-touch, human-centered practice to serve this gap. Real advisors. Real relationships. Real plans tailored to real lives. It worked beautifully.

It also didn't scale.

The question: How do you grow a business whose value proposition is human attention—without destroying the thing that makes it valuable?

The System

I came in as Fractional CXO to answer that question. The answer wasn't "add technology." It was "design a system that lets humans do what only humans can do."

Business Strategy

Defined the competitive position: not robos (efficiency, commodity), not big firms (scale through standardization), but high-touch, relevant, human service. This became the filter. If an initiative supported human service at scale, it was a contender. If it replaced human service with automation, into the icebox.

Brand Strategy

Stash Wealth's voice was already its greatest asset—sassy, direct, human. I codified it. Developed the theme and direction, worked with a brand designer to extrapolate a design system. The website, the software, the collateral—all expressions of one voice.

Technology Strategy

Identified where technology was creating friction for humans (advisors re-typing data, clients struggling with document signing) and removed it. Then designed the components that would let advisors do more, not be replaced:

  • Money Map: Visualizes income distribution for clients
  • Magic Number: Displays discretionary income—money that can be spent without compromising goals

These aren't robo-advisor features. They're tools that make human conversations more powerful.

What This Enables

The old model: human service is expensive. To scale, you either raise minimums (exclude people) or automate (commoditize).

The new model: design systems that multiply human capacity. Technology handles data ingestion, document flow, visualization. Humans handle judgment, empathy, accountability.

Stash Wealth can serve more HENRYs without becoming less human. The system scales. The service doesn't dilute.

The Difference

A conventional case study shows the website and the Money Map interface.

This one is about the layer underneath: a business strategy that filters decisions, a brand system that expresses voice, a technology architecture that amplifies humans instead of replacing them.
